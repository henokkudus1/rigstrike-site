How this math works

IFTA settles fuel tax across every state you drove in, based on where you burned the fuel, not where you bought it. The formula the calculator runs:

  • Fleet MPG = total miles ÷ total gallons purchased (across all states).
  • Taxable gallons for a state = that state's miles ÷ your fleet MPG. This is the fuel you burned in that state.
  • Net taxable gallons = taxable gallons − gallons you actually bought in that state. Positive means you owe; negative means you already paid too much there (a credit).
  • Tax = net taxable gallons × that state's diesel rate.
  • Surcharge (KY & VA): surcharge = taxable gallons × the surcharge rate, added on top. There is no purchase credit against a surcharge — you pay it on every gallon burned there.
  • Net total = the sum of every state's tax and surcharge. A negative net total is an overall credit.

Note for Q2 2026: Indiana no longer has a separate diesel surcharge — it was folded into Indiana's base diesel rate. Oregon's IFTA diesel rate is $0.00 (Oregon uses a separate weight-mile tax).
