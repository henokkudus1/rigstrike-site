IFTA Due Dates 2026

IFTA returns are filed quarterly. Each covers a three-month period and is due the last day of the month after the quarter ends. If that date falls on a weekend or legal holiday, the deadline moves to the next business day.

QuarterPeriod coveredFiling deadline
Q1 2026Jan 1 – Mar 31, 2026April 30, 2026
Q2 2026Apr 1 – Jun 30, 2026July 31, 2026
Q3 2026Jul 1 – Sep 30, 2026November 2, 2026 (Oct 31 is a Saturday)
Q4 2026Oct 1 – Dec 31, 2026February 1, 2027 (Jan 31 is a Sunday)
The zero-report rule: you must file an IFTA return every quarter while your license is active — even if you drove no miles and bought no fuel. A missed zero report can still trigger penalties and license suspension.

Common questions

Do I file if I had no miles?

Yes — file a zero report. Silence is not the same as filing.

What if I'm late?

Expect a penalty of $50 or 10% of net tax due (whichever is greater), plus per-jurisdiction interest. Repeated lateness risks suspension.

Which rates do I use?

The matrix for the quarter you're filing. For the July 31, 2026 return, use Q2 2026 rates.

Deadlines that fall on a weekend/holiday roll to the next business day. Always confirm with your base jurisdiction, since state-specific holidays can shift a date.
